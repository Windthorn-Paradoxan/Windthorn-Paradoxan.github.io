Reality as Polarity-Based

Through the EMx lens, reality isn't built on "things" (particles, objects, binary states)— it's built on polarities in relationship. The signed zeros {-0, 0, +0} express this: not positive vs negative as opposites, but positive and negative as directions of relationship to the center.

The Three Fundamental Polarities

1. Directional Polarity: ±0 (Approaching vs Receding)

+0: Approaching from below, expanding, manifesting, yang energy
-0: Approaching from above, contracting, returning, yin energy
0: The crossing point, the NULL, the moment of transition

Example: Water at freezing point—coming from liquid (+0°C) or ice (-0°C) determines what happens next, even though temperature reads "zero."

2. Dimensional Polarity: Coordinates (x, y, z)

Each axis represents a dimension of relationship:
x-axis: Horizontal/spatial position (here ↔ there)
y-axis: Vertical/energetic level (up ↔ down)
z-axis: Temporal/phase cycle (forward ↔ backward)

Every state (x,y,z) is a position in relationship space, not an isolated point.

3. Operational Polarity: Transformation Pairs

The 10 operators work in complementary pairs:
O₁ (Δ temporal) ↔ O₁₀ (Σ integration) = Rate vs Accumulation
O₂ (∇ gradient) ↔ O₃ (⤸ circulation) = Potential vs Flow
O₄ (∮ closure) ↔ O₈ (W winding) = Path vs Index
O₅ (Π projection) ↔ O₇ (S symmetry) = Collapse vs Exchange
O₆ (N normalize) ↔ O₉ (I no-clone) = Return vs Uniqueness

Each pair is polar: neither exists without the other, together they define complete action.
