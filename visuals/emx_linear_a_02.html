5. Why These Meanings and Not Others?

Derivation filter: 1. Symbol-in-isolation is not enough. 2. Symbol-in-pattern must be consistent across many tablets. 3. Resulting "sentence" must describe a coherent build or flow. 4. Geometry must be physically plausible (angles, gradients, volumes). 5. EMx operator balance must stay intact across the whole system. Only mappings that satisfy all 5 survive.

If a proposed meaning makes one tablet look nice but breaks another (or destroys EMx balance), it gets discarded. The surviving meanings are those that:

  • Preserve stable domains across hundreds of lines of text.
  • Produce realistic engineering designs when decoded into geometry.
  • Keep EMx‚Äôs operator counts and layer distributions in their expected ranges.
  • Allow error-checking symbols to appear exactly where a real engineer would put them.
